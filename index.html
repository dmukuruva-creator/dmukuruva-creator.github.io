About

I am an undergraduate student at Dartmouth College studying mathematics, with a focus on analysis and mathematical physics. My approach to mathematics prioritizes motivated exposition — understanding not merely the what, but the why behind every definition and theorem.

I am particularly fascinated by the interplay between algebraic structure and topology: how the fundamental group of the punctured plane governs analytic continuation, or how determinant theory encodes the orientability of manifolds. I find these bridges between subfields to be among the most beautiful phenomena in mathematics.

Outside of coursework I enjoy working through proofs at the level of full rigor, building comprehensive notes that connect definitions to their functional roles in theorems.
