About

Mathematics, Engineering, and Discovery

Mathematics and biology, at their foundations, share a language — one of structure, symmetry, and the search for pattern beneath apparent disorder.

I study both at Dartmouth, where my coursework has reached graduate complex analysis alongside upper-level work in probability and real analysis — pursued not as prerequisites, but as ends in themselves. The discipline is its own reward; the applications come later.

My research has taken me across disciplines: a summer with the Polymath Jr program investigating an open problem in the geometry of ellipses — where linear algebra, complex analysis, and classical geometry intersect in ways that are still not fully understood — and a year in the Goods Lab at Thayer School of Engineering, elucidating disease from the RNA of individual cells in patients with gynecological cancers.

Teaching is, for me, an extension of the same work. As a Physics TA and lead tutor for Dartmouth's Emerging Engineers program, I try to be the instructor I needed when I was first learning — one who insists that understanding is possible, not just memorization.

Finding Ellipses: Blaschke Products, Poncelet's Theorem, and the Numerical Range

A summer with the Polymath Jr program on an open problem in the geometry of ellipses — where the Blaschke ellipse, the Poncelet closure theorem, and the numerical range of a 2×2 matrix turn out to describe the same object. Presented with the research group at the 2026 Joint Mathematics Meetings.

Qublitz — Virtual Qubit Simulator

Quantum engineering is a discipline too often confined to expensive hardware and specialist vocabulary. Qublitz opens a laboratory to anyone with a browser — Bloch sphere visualization, microwave pulse simulation, IQ mixing, exceptional points — built for students who have been told this is not yet for them.

Apr – Jun 2025

InfantAir

Infants cannot equalize ear pressure during flight. InfantAir addresses a problem existing products have not solved — a medical device designed to mitigate the pressure differential responsible for ear barotrauma. Designed in SolidWorks; embedded control written in C.

Sep – Nov 2024

Nanomaterial Drug Delivery for Cervical Cancer

A grant proposal written as a team of three for a cervical cancer therapeutic built around nanomaterial drug delivery — designed to circumvent the shortcomings of conventional chemotherapy, grounded in systematic literature review of gynecological malignancies and state-of-the-art delivery mechanisms.
