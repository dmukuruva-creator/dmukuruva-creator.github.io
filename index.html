About

Mathematics, Engineering, and Discovery

Mathematics and biology, at their foundations, share a language — one of structure, symmetry, and the search for pattern beneath apparent disorder.

I study both at Dartmouth, pursuing graduate-level coursework in functional analysis, complex variables, and probability theory not as prerequisites, but as ends in themselves. The discipline is its own reward; the applications come later.

My research has taken me across disciplines: a summer with the Polymath Jr program investigating an open problem in the geometry of ellipses — where linear algebra, complex analysis, and classical geometry intersect in ways that are still not fully understood — and a year in the Goods Lab at Thayer School of Engineering, elucidating disease from the RNA of individual cells in patients with gynecological cancers.

Teaching is, for me, an extension of the same work. As a Physics TA and lead tutor for Dartmouth's Emerging Engineers program, I try to be the instructor I needed when I was first learning — one who insists that understanding is possible, not just memorization.
