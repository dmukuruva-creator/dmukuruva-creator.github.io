// About

I'm a junior at Dartmouth double-majoring in Mathematics and Biomedical Engineering. My mathematical work sits at the graduate level — functional analysis, probability theory, and complex variables — with a preference for motivated exposition: every definition should earn its place.

On the engineering side, I've done cancer genomics research with the Goods Lab, built a medical device for infant ear pressure trauma, and am currently a physics TA. I'm drawn to problems where mathematical structure illuminates biological systems — quantifying signaling pathways, modeling dynamical systems, or finding geometric objects hiding in data.

Outside of coursework and research, I serve as a Residential Advisor and as the Lead Physics Tutor for Dartmouth Emerging Engineers. I play chess and compete in archery.
