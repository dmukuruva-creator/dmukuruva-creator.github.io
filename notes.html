On dual-majoring

People occasionally ask whether mathematics and biomedical engineering are a strange combination. I have found that they share more vocabulary than either field typically acknowledges — "convergence," "structure," "stability" — and that mathematicians and biologists mean entirely different things by each word. This is, I think, a feature rather than a problem.

On archery

I shoot recurve bow as part of Dartmouth's archery program. This is, reliably, the most surprising thing I tell people about myself. I have not yet determined whether that says more about archery, about Dartmouth, or about what people expect of mathematics students.

On teaching introductory physics while studying functional analysis

There is something clarifying about being, simultaneously, a student of Banach algebras and a TA for introductory mechanics. It is a useful reminder that understanding and the ability to explain are not the same thing — and that the distance between them is where most of the actual teaching happens.
